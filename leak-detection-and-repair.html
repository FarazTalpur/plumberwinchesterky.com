Frequently Asked Questions

Leak Detection and Repair (LDAR) is a system to identify and fix leaks from industrial equipment, typically used in facilities handling volatile substances, to reduce emissions of harmful gases and improve safety and efficiency.

LDAR is important because it helps reduce emissions of volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and hazardous air pollutants (HAPs), thus protecting the environment, improving air quality, enhancing workplace safety, and ensuring regulatory compliance.

The frequency of LDAR procedures depends on regulatory requirements and the specific needs of the facility, but it often ranges from quarterly to annually. Regular checks help ensure timely identification and repair of any leaks.

Common methods for leak detection include infrared thermography, acoustic detection, ultrasonic detection, and the use of gas analyzers and sensors that detect specific emissions.

Failing to implement LDAR can lead to increased emissions of harmful pollutants, regulatory fines, environmental damage, increased operational costs, and potential safety hazards.
