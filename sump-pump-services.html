Frequently Asked Questions

A sump pump is a device installed in the lowest part of a basement or crawlspace that helps prevent flooding. It works by collecting excess water in a sump basin and pumping it away from the building to keep the area dry.

It is recommended to have your sump pump inspected annually to ensure it is in good working condition and to prevent any potential malfunctions that could lead to flooding.

Signs that your sump pump might need repair include unusual noises, constant running, frequent cycling, visible rust, and if it fails to start. If you notice any of these issues, it's best to contact a professional for service.

While some homeowners may be able to install a sump pump themselves, it's often recommended to hire a professional. This ensures proper installation, compliance with local codes, and optimal performance.

Regular maintenance for a sump pump includes checking for clogs in the discharge line, cleaning the sump pit, testing the pump to ensure it is operational, and replacing the battery in the backup system (if applicable).
