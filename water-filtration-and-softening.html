Frequently Asked Questions

Water filtration is the process of removing impurities and contaminants from the water, such as chemicals, bacteria, and sediment. Water softening, on the other hand, specifically targets calcium and magnesium minerals, which are responsible for water hardness, through a process typically involving ion exchange.

The frequency of filter changes depends on the type of filter and your water usage. Generally, it's recommended to change standard carbon filters every 6 months, while reverse osmosis filters should be changed annually. Always refer to your manufacturer's guidelines for the best results.

A water softener can extend the lifespan of your plumbing and appliances by preventing scale buildup, enhance the effectiveness of soaps and detergents, and provide smoother skin and shinier hair by removing hard minerals from the water.

No, a water softener primarily removes calcium and magnesium ions. To remove other impurities such as chlorine, sediment, and chemicals, you would also need a comprehensive water filtration system.

While water softened by most systems is generally safe to drink, it may have a higher sodium content. Those on a low-sodium diet should consult with a healthcare professional. For drinking purposes, it might be beneficial to combine a water softener with a filtration system that includes reverse osmosis.
