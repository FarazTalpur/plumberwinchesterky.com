Frequently Asked Questions

A slow-draining bathroom sink is often caused by a buildup of hair, soap scum, and debris in the drain. Try removing the drain stopper and clearing out any visible obstruction. For deeper issues, use a plunger or a drain snake to dislodge any clogs.

If your toilet is leaking at the base, it could be due to a worn-out wax ring or loose bolts. Turn off the water supply, drain the toilet, and inspect the bolts. If loose, tighten them. If the wax ring is damaged, you'll need to replace it to stop the leak.

A leaking shower head often drips even when it's turned off or shows a steady stream when not in use. Mineral buildup or worn-out washers are common causes. Try cleaning the shower head and replacing washers or O-rings to fix the problem.

Low water pressure can result from clogged aerators, leaks in the plumbing, or issues with the water supply line. Check and clean any clogged aerators on faucets, inspect visible plumbing for leaks, and consult with a plumber if you suspect more significant supply line issues.

To prevent pipes from freezing, insulate exposed pipes, keep bathroom cabinet doors open to let warmer air circulate around the plumbing, and let a small stream of water flow through the pipes when temperatures are extremely low. Ensure that your home is adequately heated during cold months.
